## Hitting the Road with Michelin

From August 2022 to December 2023, I got my hands dirty at Orange Bees, powering up the backend for *Michelin FixPix*—a slick little app tied into [Michelin’s OnCall](https://business.michelinman.com/oncall/tire-service) roadside assistance gig. This was a challenge that had me wrestling with **Java**, **Apache Camel**, **Vaadin**, **Velocity**, and **Spring** to help their roadside services more efficient. 

---

### The Mission: Keeping Techs Rolling

Michelin’s OnCall system is all about getting stranded drivers back on the road, fast—24/7, coast-to-coast, no excuses. FixPix fits right in, giving roadside techs real-time tools to handle breakdowns in the middle of nowhere. I was the guy behind the scenes, building a backend that delivered **locations**, **data points**, and quick comms to those techs in rural spots where cell service is dicey. It took some serious technical know-how to untangle the mess of requirements and lead the charge—think of it like threading a needle while the truck’s still rolling.

> **Real Talk**: Michelin’s been at this game for a while—[their OnCall service](https://michelinmedia.com/michelin-oncall-emergency-roadside-service-surpasses-25-million-breakdown-events/) has handled over 2.5 million breakdowns since 2023. FixPix is the cherry on top, streamlining tire fixes with tech I helped shape.

---

### Tools of the Trade

Here’s what I brought to the table:
- **Java**: The backbone, keeping everything steady and scalable.
- **Apache Camel**: Routing data like a pro—think of it as the GPS for our integrations.
- **Vaadin**: Made the UI side play nice with the backend, no fuss.
- **Velocity**: Templating that kept things flexible and fast.
- **Spring**: The glue holding it all together, rock-solid as always.

This wasn’t just plugging in frameworks—it was about making them work *together* under pressure, delivering for Michelin’s techs and drivers counting on us.

---

### Why It Mattered

FixPix tied into Michelin’s big OnCall push, supporting live roadside crews who needed info pronto. I’m proud of how it turned out; it’s not every day you get to boost a tire giant’s workflow while helping folks stuck on the highway. Check out [Michelin’s take](https://business.michelinman.com/oncall/tire-service) on their roadside services—FixPix is part of that legacy now, and I had a hand in it.
