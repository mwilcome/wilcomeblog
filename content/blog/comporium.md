## Diving into Comporium’s Telephone/Internet/Security Sales

Back at Orange Bees, I worked with [Comporium](https://www.comporium.com/), a Southeast company who work on phone, internet, TV, and security services across the Carolinas. I was tasked with building and supporting a fleet of **Java microservices** to power their sales pipelines—an internal tool that sales reps lean on to wrestle orders into shape. It was about streamlining a tangled mess into something sleek, all while juggling the ins and outs of their massive product lineup.

---

### The Challenge: From Chaos to Cart Clarity

Comporium’s sales system was a beast when I got there. It had thousands of fields in a single cart object I had to dig through. I stepped in to overhaul the backend, crafting pipelines that handled everything—**document generation**, **payments**, **user accounts**, you name it. The goal was to make it quick and clean for reps, syncing up with their phone, internet, and security features without dropping the ball on performance or safety.

> **Behind the Scenes**: Ever wonder how a company keeps rural internet humming or secures smart homes? Check out [Comporium’s story](https://www.comporium.com/about-us/)—my work helped their sales crew keep that machine running.

---

### Why It Hit Home

This wasn’t some faceless project. Comporium’s a name folks in the Southeast know—small enough to care, big enough to matter. I loved helping them turn their sales chaos into a backend that reps could use better, cutting through the clutter to deliver orders fast. It’s the kind of practical win that keeps the lights on (and the internet flowing) for their customers.
