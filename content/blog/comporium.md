## Diving into Comporium’s Southeast Empire

Back at Orange Bees, I took on a gig with [Comporium](https://www.comporium.com/), a Southeast staple slinging phone, internet, TV, and security services across the Carolinas. My mission? Build and support a fleet of **Java microservices** to power their sales pipelines—an internal tool that sales reps lean on to wrestle orders into shape. This wasn’t just coding; it was about streamlining a tangled mess into something sleek, all while juggling the ins and outs of their massive product lineup.

---

### The Challenge: From Chaos to Cart Clarity

Comporium’s sales system was a beast when I got there. Picture this: a cart object so bloated it could hold **thousands of fields**—a database nightmare that made order processing feel like herding cats. I stepped in to overhaul the backend, crafting pipelines that handled everything—**document generation**, **payments**, **user accounts**, you name it. The goal was to make it quick and clean for reps, syncing up with their phone, internet, and security features without dropping the ball on performance or safety.

> **Behind the Scenes**: Ever wonder how a company keeps rural internet humming or secures smart homes? Check out [Comporium’s story](https://www.comporium.com/about-us/)—my work helped their sales crew keep that machine running.

---

### My Toolbox: Skills That Delivered

Here’s what I brought to the table:
- **Java**: The rock-solid core driving it all.
- **Microservices**: Chopped that monster cart into bite-sized, scalable pieces.
- **Open API**: Kept the data flowing smooth between systems.
- **Agile**: Moved fast, iterated smart—kept the team in sync.

Balancing a massive database with real-time order needs took some serious finesse, but I made it work—streamlined, secure, and ready for action.

---

### Why It Hit Home

This wasn’t some faceless project. Comporium’s a name folks in the Southeast know—small enough to care, big enough to matter. I got a kick out of turning their sales chaos into a backend that reps could actually use, cutting through the clutter to deliver orders fast. It’s the kind of practical win that keeps the lights on (and the internet flowing) for their customers.
